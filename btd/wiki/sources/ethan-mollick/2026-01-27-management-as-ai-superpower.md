# Management as AI superpower

Mollick ran an experiment: put executive MBA students (doctors, managers, operators — few coders) in a room for four days and have them build working startup prototypes using Claude Code and Gemini Antigravity. The output was an order of magnitude beyond what a full semester produced pre-AI. The key insight: the students who thrived were those who could scope a problem, define deliverables, and recognize when output was wrong — not those with AI expertise. Management skills turn out to be the hard skills for the AI era.

## Key Ideas
- The delegation equation: delegate to AI when human baseline time is high, AI success probability is high, and evaluation cost is manageable
- GPT-5.2 beats human experts 72% of the time on 7-hour expert tasks — the math strongly favors delegation when you're a domain expert
- Good delegation formats (PRDs, Five Paragraph Orders, design briefs) are exactly what makes AI produce better output — they're the same problem
- What's scarce in an AI-abundant world is knowing what to ask for, not the ability to produce it
- The people who thrive will be those who know what "good" looks like and can explain it clearly enough for an AI to deliver it
