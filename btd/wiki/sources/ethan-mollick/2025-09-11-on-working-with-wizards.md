# On Working with Wizards

Mollick documents a shift in how AI works that changes the human role: we're moving from co-intelligence (collaborative, back-and-forth, corrective) to "wizard" mode (give a vague prompt, receive a sophisticated output you didn't participate in making). GPT-5 Pro found an error in his published academic paper in 9 minutes; Claude transformed a multi-tab Excel teaching exercise into a new business context from a single instruction. The outputs are good — but the process is opaque, and you can't verify what you can't reproduce.

## Key Ideas
- The wizard model produces impressive outputs but removes human agency from the process — you receive, you don't collaborate
- Accepting "good enough" without full verification becomes the new norm; perfect checking is increasingly impossible
- Every time you hand work to a wizard, you lose a chance to build the expertise needed to evaluate its output
- New literacy needed: know when to summon the wizard vs. when to work collaboratively vs. when not to use AI
- Competence and opacity rise together — the better the magic, the harder to detect when it's wrong
