# Three Years from GPT-3 to Gemini 3

Mollick marks three years from ChatGPT's launch by reviewing Gemini 3 — not through benchmarks, but by demonstrating the distance traveled. In 2022, AI could write a poem about otters. In 2025, it builds a playable game from a prompt. He focuses on Gemini 3's agentic coding tool (Antigravity), which operates via an Inbox metaphor: assign tasks, check in when stuck, deliver results. The most revealing test: tasking Gemini 3 to generate an original academic paper from a decade-old messy dataset, including hypothesis generation, statistical analysis, and write-up — a "PhD-level" result with PhD-level limitations.

## Key Ideas
- The era of chatbot is giving way to the era of digital coworker — "human in the loop" shifts from fixing mistakes to directing work
- Coding ability is general-purpose: anything on a computer is ultimately code, so coding agents can do almost any knowledge work
- Agentic tools with an Inbox or similar interface feel like managing a teammate, not prompting an AI
- Even at "PhD-level" output, AI has human-like weaknesses: methodology gaps, over-theorizing, needs guidance to improve
- Three years of progress: from paragraph-coherence to autonomous research and fully functional games
