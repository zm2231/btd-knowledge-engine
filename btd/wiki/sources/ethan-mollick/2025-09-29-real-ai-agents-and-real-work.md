# Real AI Agents and Real Work

AI agents have crossed a threshold: they can now perform economically valuable, expert-level work autonomously. Mollick demonstrates this by having Claude reproduce a published academic paper from raw data — converting STATA to Python, running the analysis, verifying results — in minutes rather than hours. OpenAI's GDPval benchmark shows AI completing 4–7 hour expert tasks competitively with human experts. The risk isn't just job displacement; it's thoughtlessly using AI to do more of the same work (17 PowerPoints nobody needed) rather than asking what work is worth doing.

## Key Ideas
- AI agents are self-correcting; even small accuracy gains lead to exponential gains in task completion length
- AI loses to experts on formatting and instruction-following, not knowledge — areas that are rapidly improving
- The reproduction crisis in academia could be addressed at scale: AI can verify published findings that humans couldn't check in bulk
- AI does tasks, not jobs; your job is a bundle of many tasks, most of which AI can't fully replace yet
- The key judgment call is: delegate to AI when baseline time is high, probability of success is high, and evaluation cost is manageable
