# Mass Intelligence

Mollick argues we've crossed into a new era: powerful AI is now as accessible as a Google search, with over a billion weekly users. The shift is driven by two forces collapsing at once — cost (from $50/million tokens to cents) and complexity (model selection and prompting tricks no longer required). Free users now access models that outperform experts on hard benchmarks, and the "auto" router paradigm means people get strong models without knowing to ask.

## Key Ideas
- Cost per token has dropped ~99% since GPT-4; capability has risen sharply over the same period
- Less than 7% of paying ChatGPT users selected the best model before GPT-5 auto-routing — most people were missing the best tools
- Prompt engineering techniques like chain-of-thought no longer improve results on modern models; just ask naturally
- Mass intelligence creates societal instability: every institution (schools, courts, hospitals) was built for scarce, expensive intelligence
- The era raises unsolved questions about trust, expertise, and what happens when a billion people can fabricate anything
