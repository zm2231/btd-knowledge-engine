# Giving your AI a Job Interview

Standard AI benchmarks (MMLU, GPQA) are unreliable guides for choosing which AI to use for your specific work — they measure the wrong things, contain errors, and don't reflect how models perform on real tasks. Mollick argues for "vibe benchmarking" (idiosyncratic tests that reveal a model's character and biases) combined with task-specific benchmarking modeled on OpenAI's GDPval study. Different models give consistently different advice on judgment calls, and those differences compound at scale when AI is advising thousands of decisions.

## Key Ideas
- Public benchmarks are contaminated by training data leakage and test questions that don't predict real-world usefulness
- Models differ consistently in how risk-tolerant, critical, or sycophantic they are — not just in accuracy
- "Vibe benchmarking" (writing prompts, code challenges, creative tasks) reveals personality and bias patterns that matter for your use case
- Organizations should test AI on the actual work it will do, run multiple times, evaluated by domain experts — not benchmark scores
- You need to re-evaluate models several times per year as new versions release
