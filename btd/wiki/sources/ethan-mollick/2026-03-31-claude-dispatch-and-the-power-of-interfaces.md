# Claude Dispatch and the Power of Interfaces

A research paper found that chatbot interfaces actively increase cognitive load for professional tasks — walls of text, unsolicited tangents, compounding disorganization. AI capability has been running ahead of AI accessibility, and most "AI disappointment" comes from bad interfaces, not bad models. Mollick surveys emerging interface paradigms: specialized tools (Stitch for design, Pomelli for marketing, NotebookLM for research), personal agents (OpenClaw, Claude Cowork with Dispatch), and on-demand interfaces where the AI generates the right UI for the moment. Claude Dispatch lets you send tasks from your phone while Claude works on your desktop — effectively a remote control for an AI agent.

## Key Ideas
- The chatbot interface is a constraint, not a feature — it creates cognitive overhead that cancels out AI gains, especially for less experienced users
- Claude Dispatch + Cowork represents the personal agent paradigm: familiar messaging interface, AI does real work on your actual files
- On-demand interfaces (AI generates the right chart, app, or visualization contextually) may be the most flexible long-term answer
- Specialized interfaces for non-coding professions are just beginning — Stitch, Pomelli, and NotebookLM are early sketches
- The best interface is no interface: an agent in your existing apps doing work with your actual tools
