# An Opinionated Guide to Using AI Right Now

Mollick's periodic practical guide, grounded in OpenAI's data on what people actually use ChatGPT for. Covers how to pick among free vs. paid models, which specific model tiers to select within ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini, when Deep Research is worth triggering, and how multimodal inputs (voice, camera, screen sharing) unlock new use cases. The core advice: don't optimize prompts — use agent models for real work, connect AI to your actual data, and build intuition through experimentation rather than reading guides.

## Key Ideas
- Always manually select the thinking/agent model tier for real work; default "auto" routes to weaker models
- Deep Research produces citation-accurate reports competitive with professional information services — a key underused feature
- Connecting AI to your data (email, calendar, files) is more powerful than prompting alone; Claude leads here
- Sycophancy is a real risk: explicitly ask for critic mode when you need honest feedback
- The future of AI isn't better models — it's users figuring out what AI should do
