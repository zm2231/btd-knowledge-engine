# The Book That Changed My Financial Life

A book club breakdown of Morgan Housel's *The Psychology of Money*, organized into four themes: attitudes toward money (everyone's context shapes their financial beliefs), getting money (compounding and savings rate), spending money (buy time and freedom, not status), and protecting money (leave room for error, avoid extreme commitments). Ali's key personal takeaways are around sufficiency (learning to say "this is enough"), the savings rate as the only controllable variable, and why staying in the market — even through crashes — beats trying to time it.

## Key Ideas
- Money attitudes are shaped by when and where you grew up — don't judge others' behavior without their context
- Compounding is exponential: small differences in savings rate or time horizon produce enormous differences at scale
- "Enough" is the most important financial concept — the goalpost keeps moving unless you define it deliberately
- True wealth is the money you haven't spent; spending on status buys you the object, not the admiration
- Be reasonable, not rational — decisions that help you sleep at night have real value even if they're not optimal on paper
