# How to Build Your Ultimate Productivity System

Ali walks through a three-layer productivity stack he calls Ron, Hermione, and Dumbledore. Layer one covers the four essentials every person needs: a calendar, an email system, a to-do list, and file management. Layer two adds media capture tools (Kindle, Instapaper, Readwise) for people who consume a lot of content and want to retain it. Layer three introduces "building a second brain" using apps like Roam — turning the system into a creative thought partner rather than just a storage mechanism. The core principle throughout is that your brain is for having ideas, not for holding them.

## Key Ideas
- Offload low-value memory tasks to systems so your brain is free for creative work
- Three-layer stack: life management (calendar, email, tasks, files) → capture (highlights, notes) → second brain (connections, creativity)
- 100% coverage of your to-do list is necessary — partial systems still create low-level anxiety
- "One touch to inbox zero": every email either becomes a calendar event, a task, a reference note, or gets archived
- Parkinson's Law applies to the whole system: constrain it or it expands to consume all your time
