# 21 Life Lessons for Teenagers

Ali delivers 21 pieces of advice to his 18-year-old self, framed as letters from the future. The lessons span identity (question which game you're playing and why), compounding skills (posture, active recall, investing start early), social dynamics (be the connector, expand surface area for serendipity), and mindset (ignore sunk costs, seek forgiveness not permission, stop caring what others think). Underneath the advice format is a coherent model: most people optimize for the wrong metrics because they never step back to ask what game they're actually playing.

## Key Ideas
- Question your "victory conditions" — are you still playing the game you chose at 16, or the one you actually want?
- Seek skills that compound over years, not weeks (posture, active recall, investing, building in public)
- Increase your surface area for serendipity by showing up to things and being a connector
- Sunk cost fallacy applies to career and education choices — prior decisions don't obligate future ones
- Share work openly and collaborate; university-era competitors are future colleagues
