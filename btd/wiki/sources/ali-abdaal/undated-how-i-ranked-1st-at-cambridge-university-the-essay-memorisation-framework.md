# How I ranked 1st at Cambridge University - The Essay Memorisation Framework

Ali details the two-phase system he used to win the Cambridge psychology prize: a creation phase (building first-class essay plans from research, not just lecture notes) and a memorization phase (committing those plans to memory using Anki flashcards, spider diagrams, and a retrospective revision timetable). The creation phase emphasizes structure, answering the question directly, and a bit of flair in the introduction. The memorization phase uses active recall exclusively — drawing spider diagrams from memory, checking gaps, and repeating over weeks until the content is solid.

## Key Ideas
- Creation + memorization are separate stages — mixing them produces mediocre results in both
- Start essay research from Google and review papers, not lecture notes — produces more original material
- Anki flashcards for individual content blocks let you reconstruct novel essays from parts, not just regurgitate memorized ones
- Spider diagrams from memory are better than re-reading — forces active recall, surfaces gaps
- Retrospective revision timetable schedules review based on what you actually studied, not what you planned to
