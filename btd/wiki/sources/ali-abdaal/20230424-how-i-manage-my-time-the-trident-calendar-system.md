# How I Manage My Time - The Trident Calendar System

Ali presents his Trident Method: three interlocking time management tools used at annual, weekly, and daily scales. The annual layer uses a "year at a glance" Google Sheet to keep macro context on the full year. The weekly layer is an "ideal week" calendar template — a default allocation of the 112 waking hours per week across work, health, and relationships — built after working with an executive coach. The daily layer follows the calendar and adds three "daily quests" in work, health, and relationships. The underlying insight is that without an explicit default structure, every day requires fresh decisions about how to spend time, which is exhausting and leads to drift.

## Key Ideas
- Year-at-a-glance view forces you to see your actual available time before committing to anything
- Ideal week exercise reveals that "I don't have time" is really "I'm not choosing to make time"
- Designing containers (blocks) before the week starts removes daily decision fatigue
- Three daily quests (work, health, relationships) surface the things that actually matter vs. what's urgent
- Structure enables flexibility: deviations are visible against a baseline rather than invisible in chaos
