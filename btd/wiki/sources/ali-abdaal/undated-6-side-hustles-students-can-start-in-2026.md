# 6 Side Hustles Students Can Start in 2026

Ali frames side hustles not as quick money schemes but as a skill in themselves — the skill of providing value to people who will pay for it. He distinguishes three business types (services, products, content), explains J-curve vs. non-J-curve businesses, and walks through six specific hustles: writing/newsletters, content curation, thumbnail design, research assistant, remote sales rep, and web design. The deeper argument is that high-friction service businesses are faster to monetize than content businesses, and that investing in income-generating skills beats almost any index fund return for most people.

## Key Ideas
- Making money is a skill — understanding the first law (provide value people will pay for) matters more than any specific idea
- J-curve businesses (content, products) require sustained investment before any return; service businesses can pay immediately
- Three Fs for a good side hustle: flexibility, fun, finance
- "Fast lane" investing means investing in your own skills and business, not just the S&P 500
- Getting started matters more than finding the "perfect" idea — launch, then iterate
