# What happened to these two brothers? #shorts

A ~60-second short retelling a parable from *The Millionaire Fast Lane* by MJ DeMarco. Two brothers race to build a pyramid: one starts laying bricks immediately, the other spends two years building a crane. The brick-layer exhausts himself and dies; the crane-builder finishes easily. The moral is leverage — doing more with less by investing time in systems and tools before grinding on the task itself. Applied to builders: before you build, consider what tools or systems would make the building dramatically easier.

## Key Ideas
- Leverage beats effort: investing time in a better tool or system often outperforms grinding directly on a task
- Short-term effort without leverage is physically finite; leverage compounds
- The parable illustrates the difference between working in your business vs. working on it
