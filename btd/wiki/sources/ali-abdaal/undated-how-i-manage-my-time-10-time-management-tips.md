# How I Manage My Time - 10 Time Management Tips

A practical ten-tip roundup distilled from a decade of reading productivity books and experimenting. Key tips include: own all of your time (you're always doing what you most want), use the "hell yeah or no" filter (Derek Sivers), set a daily highlight, use time blocking for at least the highlight, apply Parkinson's Law via artificial deadlines, protect your mornings, delegate below your hourly rate, automate scheduling with Calendly, and choose to feel satisfied at the end of the day. Less a system than a menu of verified practices, each with a clear mechanism.

## Key Ideas
- You always have time for what you choose — reframe "I don't have time" as "I'm not choosing to prioritize this"
- Daily highlight: one thing that must get done, scheduled as a time block
- Parkinson's Law: without artificial deadlines, work expands to fill whatever time you give it
- Protect mornings from external obligations to preserve creative and deep work capacity
- Delegating anything below your effective hourly rate is a rational productivity move
