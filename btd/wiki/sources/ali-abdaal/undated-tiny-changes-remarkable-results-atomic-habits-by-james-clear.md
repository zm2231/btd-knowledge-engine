# Tiny Changes, Remarkable Results - Atomic Habits by James Clear

Ali's book club summary of *Atomic Habits*, distilling James Clear's four key insights. First, 1% daily improvements compound to 37x better in a year — but the plateau of latent potential means results lag behind effort, causing most people to quit too soon. Second, systems beat goals because winners and losers often have the same goals; systems are what differentiate them. Third, identity change is the most durable foundation for habit change — "I am a writer" beats "I will write." Fourth, the four laws of behavior change (make it obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying) provide a practical design framework for any habit.

## Key Ideas
- 1% better per day for a year = 37x improvement; 1% worse = near zero — compounding cuts both ways
- Plateau of latent potential: progress is happening before it's visible; quitting at the plateau is the main failure mode
- Focus on systems, not goals — the goal is to keep playing the game, not to win it once
- Identity-based habits: "what would a healthy person do?" is more durable than "I want to lose weight"
- Four laws: make cues obvious, make habits attractive, reduce friction (make it easy), add immediate rewards
