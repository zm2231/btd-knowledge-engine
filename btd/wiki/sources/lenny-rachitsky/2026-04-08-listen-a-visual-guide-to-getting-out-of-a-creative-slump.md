# Listen: A visual guide to getting out of a creative slump

Audio version of Michelle Rial's visual pep talk, delivered via the Lenny's Reads podcast. Michelle walks through her 12-step guide for getting out of a creative block in spoken form, making it useful for people who want the content while commuting or away from a screen. She covers why it's necessary to create embarrassing work, the mindset shift that stops stalling, how to reframe scorn as a positive signal, and techniques for moving out of rumination. Timed to celebrate the launch of her children's book *Charts for Babies*.

## Key Ideas
- Create work that might embarrass you — discomfort signals you're doing something real
- Stop waiting to feel ready; starting is the action that generates readiness
- Scorn and criticism from others often signals your work is distinct — reframe it as confirmation of differentiation
- Rumination loops are broken by movement and small action, not by thinking harder
- The fear that AI makes human creativity irrelevant is a creative block like any other — make the work anyway
