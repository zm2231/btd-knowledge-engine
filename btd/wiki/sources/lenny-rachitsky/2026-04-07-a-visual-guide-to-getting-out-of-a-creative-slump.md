# A visual guide to getting out of a creative slump

Guest post by Michelle Rial (cartoonist, author of *Charts for Babies*) timed to her book launch. A visual pep talk for anyone stuck in a creative rut — particularly resonant in a moment when AI is making creators question whether human creative work still matters. Rial's 12-step guide uses her signature hand-drawn chart format to walk through practical mindset shifts: start before you're ready, create work that embarrasses you, reframe scorn as signal, stop ruminating and move. The underlying message is that the discomfort of creating is the work, not a sign something is wrong.

## Key Ideas
- Create work that feels embarrassing — the embarrassment is evidence you're doing something real, not safe
- Start before you feel ready; the act of starting generates the momentum that motivation was supposed to provide
- Scorn from others is often a signal your work is distinct enough to threaten — reframe it as confirmation
- Rumination is the enemy of creation; movement (literally or figuratively) breaks the loop
- AI threatening creative work is the newest version of a centuries-old anxiety — humans kept creating anyway
