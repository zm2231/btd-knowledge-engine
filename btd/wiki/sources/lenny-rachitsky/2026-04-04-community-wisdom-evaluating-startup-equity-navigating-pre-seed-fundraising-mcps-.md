# Community Wisdom: Evaluating startup equity, navigating pre-seed fundraising, MCPs vs. CLIs, Monzo's U.S. exit, and more

Weekly digest of the highest-signal threads from Lenny's members-only Slack community. This edition surfaces practitioner wisdom on four topics: how to evaluate startup equity offers (dilution, strike price, liquidation preferences), how to navigate pre-seed fundraising in the current market, when to use MCPs vs. CLIs for AI tooling decisions, and lessons from Monzo's failed U.S. expansion. The format is community-sourced intelligence — practitioners sharing what actually happened, not what the theory says should happen.

## Key Ideas
- Equity evaluation requires modeling dilution across multiple future rounds, not just the current grant percentage
- Pre-seed fundraising success correlates strongly with warm intros and clear differentiated thesis, not deck quality
- MCPs are better for persistent, stateful AI interactions; CLIs are better for scripted, deterministic workflows
- Monzo's U.S. exit illustrates how product-market fit in one geography doesn't transfer automatically
- Community wisdom as a format: high-signal because it's based on actual decisions made under real constraints
