# An AI state of the union: We've passed the inflection point, dark factories are coming, and automation timelines | Simon Willison

Simon Willison (Django co-creator, coined "prompt injection" and "AI slop") gives a candid state-of-the-union on AI's impact on software builders. His thesis: November 2025 was the inflection point where AI coding agents crossed from "mostly works" to "actually works." He now writes 95% of his code from his phone and is mentally exhausted by 11am. He identifies three agentic engineering patterns (red/green TDD, templates, hoarding), warns that mid-career engineers — not juniors — are most at risk, and describes the coming "dark factory" pattern where AI does its own QA without human review.

## Key Ideas
- November 2025 marked the inflection point — AI coding is now reliable enough to build on in production
- Mid-career engineers are more at risk than juniors because AI replicates their pattern-matching most faithfully
- Three agentic engineering patterns: red/green TDD (write tests first, let AI make them pass), templates (AI-generated scaffolds), hoarding (saving every useful prompt/pattern)
- Dark factory pattern: AI writes code, runs tests, reviews its own output — humans only set direction
- Prompt injection remains an unsolved security problem; the "lethal trifecta" could cause an AI Challenger disaster
