The AI scare trade hit 8 sectors in 10 days, creating a panicking market that doesn't know which companies are truly exposed. Three categories: displaced today, 3–5 year horizon, market lost the plot. The domain translator — someone who bridges deep domain expertise with AI capability — is the most valuable role in a panicking organization. The career split is between people who can bridge that gap and those who can't.

## Key Ideas

- AI scare trade creates noise: 8 sectors affected in 10 days, but most market reactions conflate the three exposure categories
- Domain translator role = most valuable in organizations that understand AI is coming but don't know how to use it
- Three exposure categories require different responses; treating them the same is the common mistake
- Career split is not AI vs non-AI — it's bridge-builders vs specialists who can only work within their domain
- The opportunity is largest where panic is highest and clarity is scarcest
