K-shaped job market with 3.2:1 jobs-to-candidates ratio on the high-value side. Based on actual job postings, seven skills define the high side: specification precision, evaluation and quality judgment, multi-agent coordination, failure pattern recognition (six failure types), trust and security design, context architecture, and token economics. These aren't soft skills — they're learnable technical disciplines that most practitioners have never been taught.

## Key Ideas

- K-shaped split: high-value AI roles can't fill fast enough; commoditized roles are collapsing — simultaneously
- 3.2:1 jobs-to-candidates ratio for high-value AI work; extracted from real job postings, not projections
- Seven skills from postings: spec precision, eval judgment, multi-agent coordination, failure recognition, trust design, context architecture, token economics
- Six failure pattern types that practitioners need to recognize and debug in agent systems
- The skills are learnable; the gap is that 201-level training barely exists yet
