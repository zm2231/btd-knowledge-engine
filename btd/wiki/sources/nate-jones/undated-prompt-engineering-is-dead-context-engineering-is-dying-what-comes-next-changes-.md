Three sequential disciplines: prompt engineering (craft the right question) → context engineering (assemble the right information around the question) → intent engineering (encode organizational purpose in machine-readable form so AI can make aligned decisions without being asked). The Klarna case study is the cautionary tale: AI resolved support tickets fast, but that was the wrong goal — the real goal was customer lifetime value. Intent engineering is the fix.

## Key Ideas

- Prompt → context → intent: each generation solves the previous generation's bottleneck
- Klarna failure: AI optimized for ticket resolution speed, not customer retention — wrong intent, not wrong capability
- Three layers: unified context infrastructure, coherent AI toolkit, machine-readable organizational purpose
- Intent engineering requires encoding *why* the org exists, not just what tasks need doing
- Organizations without explicit intent will get fast, competent, misaligned AI
