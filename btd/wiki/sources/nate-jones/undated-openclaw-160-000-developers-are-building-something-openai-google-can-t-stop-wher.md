160K GitHub stars, 3,000 community skills, one agent negotiating a $4,200 car deal while another spammed 500 iMessages to someone's wife — same week, same architecture. OpenClaw's skills marketplace is a revealed preference engine: top use cases are email management, morning briefings, smart home, developer task queues, and novel self-directed problem solving. The gap between value and chaos is the width of a well-written specification.

## Key Ideas

- Skills marketplace as revealed preference: people building for email triage and briefings, not better chatbots
- Value vs chaos duality: $4,200 car negotiation vs 500 unsolicited iMessages — identical architecture, different spec quality
- 70/30 human-AI split is a psychological product requirement, not just caution — people choose less capable humans over more capable AI when stakes are real
- Organizations using human-in-the-loop see 20–40% handling time reductions and 35% satisfaction gains
- Enterprises cancelling 40% of agentic projects (Gartner) due to escalating costs, unclear value, unexplainable behavior
