Stack: Slack + Notion + Zapier + Claude. Eight building blocks mapped to human memory functions: Dropbox (capture), Sorter (classifier), Form (schema), Filing Cabinet (Notion DB), Receipt (audit log), Bouncer (confidence filter), Tap on Shoulder (proactive nudge), Fix Button (feedback handle). 12 engineering principles govern the build. No-code friendly — the skill is designing the architecture, not writing the plumbing.

## Key Ideas

- Second brain is infrastructure, not just storage — it actively routes, filters, and surfaces information
- Eight components map to human cognitive functions: capture, classify, schema, store, audit, filter, proact, correct
- Bouncer (confidence filter) prevents the system from surfacing low-confidence information as if it were certain
- Proactive nudge (Tap on Shoulder) inverts the default: system comes to you, you don't query it
- No-code stack means the barrier is architecture thinking, not engineering skill
