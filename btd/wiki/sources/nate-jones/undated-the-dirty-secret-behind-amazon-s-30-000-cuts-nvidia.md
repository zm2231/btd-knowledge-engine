Short take (9 min). AWS is losing market share on AI workloads to Azure and Google Cloud, and the solution requires GPU capacity Amazon doesn't have. The 30K layoffs aren't AI automating those roles — they're salary savings funding Nvidia GPU procurement. The internal story (culture, performance) and the financial story (CapEx gap) are completely different, and only one of them explains the pattern.

## Key Ideas

- AWS losing AI workload competition to Azure and Google Cloud — the gap is GPU infrastructure
- 30K cuts = ~$6B saved → redirected to Nvidia GPU procurement, not headcount optimization
- Automation narrative is false here: the cuts fund computing, not replace workers with AI
- CapEx vs headcount tradeoff is the real strategic decision, obscured by culture language
- Pairs with the $125B secret video for the full Amazon infrastructure picture
