Microsoft tracked 300K employees: AI excitement peaks at 3 weeks, then craters. The dropout isn't tool fatigue — it's the 201-level gap. Basic AI training teaches what the tools can do; nobody teaches how to manage AI as a workflow. Six skills derived from actual usage patterns: context assembly, quality judgment, task decomposition, iterative refinement, workflow integration, frontier recognition. AI is a management skill, not a tool skill.

## Key Ideas

- 3-week dropout pattern: excitement → struggle → abandonment — the tools didn't fail, the training did
- 201-level gap: 101 courses teach features; nobody teaches management of AI output quality
- Six skills: context assembly, quality judgment, task decomposition, iterative refinement, workflow integration, frontier recognition
- AI is a management skill: you're managing outputs and a workflow, not learning a software interface
- Centaur vs cyborg revisited: knowing when to switch modes vs staying fused — both require developed judgment
