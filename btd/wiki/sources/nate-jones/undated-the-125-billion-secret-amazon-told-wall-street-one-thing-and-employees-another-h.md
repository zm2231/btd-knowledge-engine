Amazon's FCF went negative while capex hit $125B, 75% directed to AI infrastructure. The layoffs of 30K employees aren't AI automating jobs — they're capital reallocation: cut $6B in salaries to fund GPU data centers. Amazon told Wall Street a growth story and told employees a culture story; neither narrative names the real driver. The pattern is: infrastructure investment is existential, headcount is variable.

## Key Ideas

- $125B capex with 75% going to AI infrastructure — not a bet, a must-have to compete with Azure and Google Cloud
- 30K layoffs = $6B salary savings funneled directly into GPU procurement
- Wall Street narrative: growth investment; employee narrative: culture reset; real story: capital reallocation
- AWS falling behind Azure and Google Cloud on AI workloads — the layoffs fund catching up
- Lesson for workers: headcount reductions in big tech are increasingly about CapEx math, not performance
