A 200-line markdown prompt from DeepSeek caused a $285B market loss — but the crash wasn't about the model. It was about the SaaS pricing model breaking: per-seat licensing dies when AI does the work instead of the seat. KPMG was already negotiating audit fee cuts using AI as leverage. The companies that survive are those that rebuild as agentic-first, not those that bolt AI onto existing per-seat products.

## Key Ideas

- $285B wasn't a panic about DeepSeek's capability — it was recognition that per-seat SaaS pricing is structurally broken
- Markdown prompt triggered the crash; the underlying data and infrastructure weren't threatened
- KPMG negotiating audit fee cuts with AI as leverage: the professional services disruption is already in motion
- Bolted-on AI extends a dying business model; agentic-first architecture rebuilds around outcomes, not seats
- The crash is a signal about business model risk, not technical capability risk
