When code cost drops to zero, specification becomes the only scarce resource. Two engineer classes emerge: high-value token drivers who define, evaluate, and architect — and low-leverage implementers who are being commoditized. All knowledge work is converging on the same bottleneck: can you write a specification good enough that an agent can execute it? Centaur (human + AI switching roles) vs cyborg (human + AI merged) as two different work patterns with different leverage points.

## Key Ideas

- Code cost → zero = specification becomes the scarce resource, not execution
- Two knowledge worker classes: those who drive AI output (high value) and those who produce what AI produces (commoditized)
- All knowledge work converging on specification: lawyers, accountants, marketers facing the same shift as developers
- Centaur vs cyborg: switching between human and AI mode vs continuous integration — different leverage, different skill requirements
- J-curve before the productivity surge: adoption looks worse before it looks better
