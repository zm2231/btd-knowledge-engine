# Google's New AI Is Smarter Than Everyone's But Costs Half as Much — Here's Why That Changes Your Stack

Gemini 3.1 Pro leads on reasoning benchmarks at roughly 1/7 the cost of comparable models, forcing a rethink of model selection. Nate's 6 problem-type taxonomy (reasoning, effort, coordination, emotional, judgment, ambiguity) gives a practical routing framework: not every task needs the smartest model, and routing correctly is itself a differentiating skill. Google is playing a different strategic game than Anthropic — distribution and cost, not safety and character.

## Key Ideas
- Gemini 3.1 Pro: strongest reasoning at ~1/7 the cost — cost is now a first-class decision variable
- 6 problem types require different models: reasoning / effort / coordination / emotional / judgment / ambiguity
- Model routing as a skill: knowing which model to send which task is real competitive advantage
- Google's strategy (distribution + cost) differs from Anthropic's (safety + character) — both matter
- The right question is "which model for this task?" not "which model overall?"
