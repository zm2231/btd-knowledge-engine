# Controlling Your Dopamine For Motivation, Focus & Satisfaction

A deep science episode on how dopamine drives pursuit, not just pleasure. Huberman explains dopamine as the engine of motivation and craving — it spikes in anticipation more than reward — and covers how modern behaviors (phone checking, layered rewards) erode the baseline. Practical protocols include deliberate cold exposure, avoiding reward stacking, and structuring effort so dopamine is tied to the process rather than the outcome.

## Key Ideas
- Dopamine governs motivation and craving, not just reward — the anticipation spike often exceeds the payoff
- Reward stacking (coffee + music + pre-workout + social media) depletes baseline dopamine over time
- The same activity produces less dopamine when you add external rewards to something you already find meaningful
- Cold water exposure reliably raises dopamine baseline for hours without the crash of stimulant-based peaks
- Attaching dopamine to effort (enjoying the grind) sustains long-term motivation better than chasing outcomes
