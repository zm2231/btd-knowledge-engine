# ADHD and How Anyone Can Improve Their Focus

Huberman explains ADHD through the lens of dopamine and norepinephrine dysregulation, then generalizes the science to focus improvement for everyone. The episode covers how ADHD brains are understimulated at baseline (not overstimulated), why stimulant medications work, and what behavioral and environmental tools — including visual focus drills, cold exposure, and deliberate attention training — can strengthen the focus circuitry regardless of diagnosis.

## Key Ideas
- ADHD reflects insufficient dopamine/norepinephrine signaling in prefrontal circuits, not excess stimulation
- Visual focus (narrowing gaze to a fixed point) directly engages the neural circuits for cognitive focus
- The prefrontal cortex suppresses distraction — building its "muscle" through deliberate practice improves focus
- Short bouts of deliberate focus training (10–15 min) are more effective than marathon concentration sessions
- Sleep, exercise, and low-stimulant environments are foundational; supplements and meds layer on top
