# Optimal Protocols for Studying and Learning

Huberman's most directly actionable episode for BTD builders: a data-driven protocol stack for studying. Covers spacing, interleaving, retrieval practice, and the "testing effect" — self-testing beats passive review by a wide margin. Addresses study environment (no phone, limited interruption), timing relative to sleep and exercise, and why teaching what you learn is one of the most powerful consolidation tools available.

## Key Ideas
- Retrieval practice (testing yourself without notes) produces stronger long-term retention than any passive review method
- Spaced repetition — returning to material at increasing intervals — is the most time-efficient learning structure
- Interleaving topics within a session feels harder but produces better transfer and retention than blocked practice
- A 20-minute nap or quiet rest after studying measurably accelerates consolidation
- Explaining material to someone else (or out loud to yourself) forces the gaps that passive review hides
