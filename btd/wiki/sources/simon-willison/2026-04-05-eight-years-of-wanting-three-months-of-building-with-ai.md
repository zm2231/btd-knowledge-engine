# Eight years of wanting, three months of building with AI

Willison highlights Lalit Maganti's deep-dive on building syntaqlite — a production-grade SQLite linter/parser — with AI assistance after eight years of procrastination. The post is unusually honest about both where AI helps (tedious implementation, overcoming inertia) and where it hurts (architectural decision-making, accumulating design debt that's hard to see until it collapses).

## Key Ideas
- AI excels at tedious, well-specified tasks (400+ grammar rules) and is excellent for getting unstuck and reaching a first prototype fast
- The first vibe-coded prototype was thrown away: AI deferred key design decisions and the architecture "corroded" clarity over time
- AI was "unhelpful to harmful" when the builder didn't know what they wanted — it can't reason toward a design that hasn't been conceived yet
- Implementation has a checkable right answer; design doesn't — this asymmetry is where AI assistance breaks down
