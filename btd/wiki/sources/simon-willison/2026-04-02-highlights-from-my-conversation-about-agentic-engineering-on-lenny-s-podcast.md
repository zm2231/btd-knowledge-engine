# Highlights from my conversation about agentic engineering on Lenny's Podcast

Simon Willison's curated highlights from his Lenny Rachitsky podcast appearance, covering the November 2025 inflection point in AI coding, the shift in software development economics, and the human skills that still matter. Essential reading for anyone trying to understand how coding agents are changing what builders actually do day-to-day.

## Key Ideas
- The November 2025 inflection point: models crossed a threshold where agent-written code works "almost all of the time" rather than "mostly" — that difference changes everything about how you can work
- The bottleneck has moved from writing code to testing and validating it; rapid prototyping is now the superpower, not implementation speed
- Using coding agents well is mentally exhausting — Willison burns out by 11am running four agents in parallel; sustainable workflows require new personal limits
- Mid-career engineers are the most at risk; senior experience amplifies AI output, and juniors get onboarding help, but the middle gets squeezed
- Responsible vibe coding: fine for personal projects, requires rigor the moment other people depend on your output
