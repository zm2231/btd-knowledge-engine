# Demis Hassabis - DeepMind AI Superintelligence and the Future of Humanity

Hassabis, CEO of DeepMind and architect of AlphaFold and AlphaZero, discusses what a meaningful benchmark for general intelligence actually looks like — arguing the Turing Test was never meant as a rigorous test — and what it would take to build systems that genuinely solve science. He describes DeepMind's approach as applying reinforcement learning and self-play to domains humans consider nearly intractable, and reflects on what superintelligence would mean for humanity's long-term trajectory.

## Key Ideas
- The Turing Test is a philosophical thought experiment, not a rigorous benchmark; a Nobel-Prize-winning AI system would be a more meaningful signal
- AlphaFold and AlphaZero demonstrate that self-play and RL can crack problems humans considered nearly impossible
- The goal is AI as a tool for scientific discovery, not just automation — an "accelerant for solving everything else"
- Superintelligence raises genuine civilizational stakes; Hassabis treats safety as foundational, not optional
- AGI development will likely be incremental, with each capability milestone revealing the next set of hard problems
