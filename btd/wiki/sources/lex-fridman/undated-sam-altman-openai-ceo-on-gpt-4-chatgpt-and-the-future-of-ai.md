# Sam Altman - OpenAI CEO on GPT-4 ChatGPT and the Future of AI

Altman's first long-form conversation with Lex covers OpenAI's origin as a mocked organization openly pursuing AGI, GPT-4's capabilities and limits, and the dual nature of the moment — transformative upside, existential downside. He treats AGI not as a distant abstraction but as a near-term engineering and governance problem, and discusses what alignment actually means in practice for a company deploying frontier models at scale.

## Key Ideas
- OpenAI was ridiculed for openly targeting AGI in 2015; the field's shift toward taking it seriously happened faster than most expected
- GPT-4 represents a qualitative leap, but Altman is careful not to overclaim — he sees it as a powerful but incomplete tool
- AGI poses simultaneous opportunities (eliminating poverty, accelerating science) and risks (totalitarian control, undermining human agency)
- Alignment isn't just a technical problem — it's entangled with institutional design, incentive structures, and political power
- The most important near-term question is not capability but governance: who checks and balances the labs building these systems
