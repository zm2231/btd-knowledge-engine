# Yann LeCun - Meta AI Open Source Limits of LLMs AGI and the Future of AI

LeCun, Meta's Chief AI Scientist and Turing Award winner, makes the case that autoregressive LLMs cannot reach human-level intelligence because they lack four essential capabilities: understanding the physical world, persistent memory, reasoning, and planning. He argues that open-source AI is not a safety risk but a civilizational safeguard against a small number of companies controlling everyone's information diet — and has been walking the walk by releasing Llama 2 and Llama 3.

## Key Ideas
- LLMs lack the four pillars of intelligence: world-model understanding, persistent memory, reasoning, and planning — making them useful but not a path to AGI
- The real danger is not runaway AI but concentration of power in a few proprietary AI systems controlling information at scale
- Open-source AI (Llama 2/3) democratizes access and empowers human goodness rather than amplifying risk
- LeCun is an explicit anti-doomer: AGI will be built, but it will be beneficial and within human control if the incentives are right
- Auto-regressive generation is a useful building block but architecturally insufficient — the field needs models that can plan and build world representations
