# Andrej Karpathy - Tesla AI Self-Driving Optimus Aliens and AGI

Karpathy walks through neural networks from first principles — matrix multiplies, trainable knobs, emergent behavior — then zooms out to Tesla's self-driving stack, Optimus, and what AGI might actually mean. He frames trained neural nets as "complicated alien artifacts," distinct from biological brains, that compress the internet's knowledge into weights through optimization. The conversation ranges from how LLMs relate to next-word prediction, to whether synthetic AI is the next stage of civilizational development.

## Key Ideas
- Neural networks are simple math (matrix multiplies + nonlinearities) that produce surprising emergent behavior when scaled and optimized hard enough
- Trained models are not analogous to the brain — they arise from compression objectives, not multi-agent evolutionary self-play
- GPTs doing next-word prediction are effectively doing lossy compression of the internet, which happens to encode deep world knowledge
- Self-driving and robotics (Optimus) are downstream applications of the same learned representation problem
- Karpathy entertains the idea that physics itself has exploits, and sufficiently capable AIs might find them
