# Group Session - AI Systems and Product

A BTD group session on using AI as a product development system rather than a task tool. The conversation centers on how to synthesize curated content (podcasts, YouTube, notes) into product ideas through automated collision — scheduled prompts that surface ideas from accumulated inputs without manual review. Max describes his approach of combining a corpus of creators' thinking, filtering it through a personal lens, and letting AI generate novel cross-domain solutions overnight.

## Key Ideas
- Creating an "environment for collisions" — feeding diverse accumulated content into AI to surface unexpected product angles — is more valuable than reactive prompting
- Scheduled autonomous prompts (e.g., nightly synthesis across a podcast library) can function as a creative morning brief more useful than calendar prep
- Cross-industry translation: finding how problems already solved in one industry apply to your domain is a repeatable ideation technique
- Building to learn vs. building to ship requires different constraints — the former tolerates ambiguity, the latter demands specificity upfront
- The constraint of who you are (what you follow, what you care about) should precede any AI-assisted build scoping
