# Group Session - Deconstructing Greatness

A BTD group session exploring how to extract transferable structure from high-performing content and people — not what they said, but how they think. Max introduces the "Deconstructing Greatness" framework (from Ron Friedman's book) as a pre-AI method of reverse-engineering excellence at the skeletal layer, then maps it onto AI-native workflows: using Claude to strip meta-patterns from any creator's work and synthesize them into personal practice. Jason adds the value of AI-generated critique and anti-pattern detection as a complement to sycophantic validation.

## Key Ideas
- Deconstructing greatness means analyzing structure, sequence, and pattern — not content — in high-performing work (talks, articles, products)
- AI makes this systematic: point it at a creator's catalog and ask for the meta-layer, not the surface ideas
- Building a "synthetic version of yourself" that surfaces blind spots and challenges assumptions is more valuable than one that validates your thinking
- Jason's end-of-session wrap-up protocol with Claude Code mines anti-patterns and gaps in his own reasoning, not just summaries
- Systems thinking applied before building prevents the common failure of building something technically functional but wrong for the actual goal
