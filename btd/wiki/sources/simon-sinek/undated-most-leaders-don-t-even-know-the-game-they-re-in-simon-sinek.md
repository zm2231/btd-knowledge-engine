# Most Leaders Don't Even Know the Game They're In | Simon Sinek

Sinek's most complete single talk: opens with the transition from "responsible for the job" to "responsible for the people responsible for the job," covers empathy in practice (the Four Seasons / Caesar's Palace Noah story), then pivots to the infinite game framework. Finite games have winners and losers; business is an infinite game. Companies playing to win (beat competitors) are systematically beaten by companies playing to stay in the game (advance their why). Ends with the Millennial section on parenting, dopamine, impatience, and environment.

## Key Ideas
- Leadership is a transition: from being responsible for the job, to being responsible for the people who do the job
- Empathy means asking "are you okay?" before "your numbers are down" — caring about the human, not the output
- The infinite game: business has no endpoint, no agreed rules, no winner — leaders who play finite ("beat competitors") are systematically outlasted by those playing infinite ("keep advancing our purpose")
- Apple vs. Microsoft: one was obsessed with competitors, one was obsessed with helping teachers and students — the infinite player frustrates the finite one
- Most corporate burnout in young workers traces to four compounding factors: failed parenting, technology/dopamine addiction, impatience for instant results, and environments that punish vulnerability
