# Celebrate the ones who DO, not the ones who WIN

A short clip built around Phil Knight's story of describing Nike at a conference. Knight asked the audience to stand based on how often they run — kept filtering until only the most dedicated remained — then said: before the sun comes up, cold and wet, running alone, "we're the ones under the lamppost cheering you on." Sinek uses this to show that "Just Do It" is about process and effort, not winning — and that celebrating doers, not winners, is what great brand stories (and leadership) look like.

## Key Ideas
- Celebrating effort over outcomes changes who you attract and motivates sustained commitment regardless of results
- Phil Knight's Nike framing reveals that the brand's core is about participation and grit, not victory
- "Just Do It" is a process statement, not a results statement — the most durable motivation is effort-based
- Leaders who celebrate doers over winners signal that showing up matters more than the scoreboard
- Narrative specificity (Knight's lamp post image) makes abstract values concrete and emotionally resonant
