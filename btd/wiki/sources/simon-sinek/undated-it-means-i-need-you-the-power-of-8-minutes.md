# It Means "I Need You": The Power of 8 Minutes

A short, emotionally direct clip about the gap between wanting help and asking for it clearly. Sinek's friend reached out several times ("want to come over?") while struggling, but Sinek didn't register it as a cry for help. Solution: they created a code phrase — "do you have 8 minutes?" — that means "I need you" without ambiguity. The insight is that ambiguous signals fail in moments of real need, and that designing explicit communication protocols for care is an act of friendship, not awkwardness.

## Key Ideas
- People in distress often communicate indirectly to avoid feeling like a burden — ambiguous signals get missed
- Explicit codes and protocols (like "do you have 8 minutes?") remove interpretation burden from both sides
- 8 minutes of focused human support is enough to shift someone's state — proximity and presence matter more than duration
- Failing to design clear communication for hard moments isn't kindness — it's avoidance that leaves people alone
- This is a leadership and team-building insight too: creating explicit signals for "I need support" reduces silent struggle
