# "Why are we friends?" | A Quick Way to #StartWithWHY

A short clip presenting a practical exercise for finding your WHY: ask a close friend "why are we friends?" and push them past the generic answers until they describe something specific about you. When they finally get there, you'll have an emotional response — goosebumps or tears — because what they've named is the value you actually provide, expressed through someone else's experience of you. This is Sinek's method for converting an abstract concept (personal WHY) into a felt, concrete realization.

## Key Ideas
- Your WHY is best discovered through how others experience you, not through self-reflection alone
- The "why are we friends?" exercise surfaces your actual values by forcing specificity from someone who lives them
- The emotional response (goosebumps, welling up) is the signal that you've hit the real thing, not a polished answer
- Starting with WHY means your purpose is defined from impact and meaning, not from roles or achievements
- This is a coaching tool: sit with the discomfort of the question, don't rescue the other person from the search
