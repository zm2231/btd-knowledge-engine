# Building With the Garage Door Open

Live problem-solving in public beats polished demos because it models the actual experience of figuring things out. The "garage door open" approach — letting others watch you work through real problems — creates an apprenticeship dynamic where discovery is personal, not instructional. This is how you build trust and create content with lasting value in the post-how-to era.

## Key Ideas
- Showing live problem-solving creates more value than polished finished output
- Discovery is personal — watching someone figure something out teaches differently than being told the answer
- The garage door metaphor: working in public invites others into your process
- Apprenticeship model replaces tutorial model when the tutorial is free from AI
- What you build in public becomes a record of your judgment, not just your output
