# Lights. Camera. Friction.

An honest stress-test of Claude Cowork as a workflow tool, not a demo. Complex multi-step tasks failed; friction forced a beginner's mind and surfaced where AI assistance actually breaks down. The takeaway reframes friction: it's not failure, it's the teacher. Cowork works best as an on-ramp to more capable tools like Claude Code, not as an endpoint.

## Key Ideas
- Complex workflows exposed real limitations; simple tasks worked well
- Friction forced a beginner's mindset that more experienced users often skip
- Cowork positioned as an on-ramp to Claude Code, not a replacement
- Honest demos — including failures — are more instructive than polished showcases
- The beginner's mind unlocked by friction is itself a skill worth practicing
