# Nobody Read the Fine Print

The washing machine paradox applied to AI: labor-saving devices reduced time spent on tasks but didn't free up time — standards rose instead. Passive AI use (delegating without engagement) follows the same pattern and adds a worse outcome: eroded self-efficacy. The argument is for staying inside the work — using AI as a collaborator you think alongside, not a service you outsource to.

## Key Ideas
- The washing machine paradox: efficiency tools raise standards instead of freeing time
- Passive AI delegation erodes self-efficacy — you become dependent without becoming capable
- "Stay inside the work" is the counter-principle: AI as collaborator, not outsourcing
- Fine print of AI tools: capability transfer requires active engagement, not passive consumption
- Learned helplessness is a real outcome of convenience-first AI adoption
