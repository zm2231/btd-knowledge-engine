# The AI Skill Nobody's Practicing

The I-35W bridge collapse analogy: the engineer who signed off without checking bore the accountability. Most people using AI are rubber-stamping outputs they didn't verify — creating the same accountability gap at scale. The Zapier 4-behavior rubric (ignore, accept, modify, reject) gives a framework for measuring how actively you're engaging. The diagnostic question: "Could I explain why this is right?"

## Key Ideas
- AI creates an accountability gap: you take credit for outputs you can't verify
- The I-35W bridge analogy — signing off without checking is professional negligence
- Zapier's 4-behavior rubric: ignore / accept / modify / reject — most users stay at accept
- "Could I explain why this is right?" is the single-question test for accountability
- Verification as a skill is trainable and currently rare — a real differentiator
