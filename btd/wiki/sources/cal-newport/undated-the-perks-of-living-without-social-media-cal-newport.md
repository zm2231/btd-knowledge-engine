# The Perks of Living Without Social Media | Cal Newport

Newport — who has never had a social media account — argues that the benefits of absence are greater than most people expect. Four perks: productive boredom (solitude that drives higher-quality leisure and self-reflection), lower anxiety (your mental model of the world is built from what you attend to), more privacy (you control what you reveal), and a more calibrated sense of self-importance (real influence from real relationships, not vanity metrics).

## Key Ideas
- Boredom is useful: without phone-based distraction, idle time becomes a pressure toward reading, exercise, and deeper connection
- Anxiety tracks your information diet — social media constructs a world of outrage and inferiority; the offline world is calmer by default
- Every day on social media chips away privacy through accumulation — you reveal far more than any single post suggests
- Platform metrics (likes, follower counts) create an inflated, manipulated sense of importance; real credibility comes from real relationships
- Newport's "disappear for 30 days without telling anyone" test: most people discover the audience they thought was watching wasn't
