# Core Idea: Time Management

Newport's three-C framework for professional time management: Capture (trusted system for tasks and plans), Configure (organized so you can see what's on your plate instantly), and Control (proactive multi-scale planning instead of reactive moment-by-moment decisions). Uses his own Trello + Google Docs + time-block planner setup as an example, and adds a fourth principle — Constrain — for managing what gets onto your plate in the first place.

## Key Ideas
- Capture: all tasks and plans live in a trusted external system, never in your head — reduces anxiety and decision fatigue
- Configure: information organized so you can get the gestalt of your plate instantly, with all context attached
- Control: multi-scale planning (quarterly/semester, weekly, daily) gives your time a job before the moment arrives
- Constrain: managing what you say yes to upstream is the hidden lever — well-designed processes reduce the overhead that reaches your system
- Time-blocking says what you will do during each available hour, not just what you need to do eventually
