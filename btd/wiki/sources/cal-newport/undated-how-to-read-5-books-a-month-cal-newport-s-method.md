# How to Read 5 Books a Month | Cal Newport's Method

Newport argues that reading is cognitive exercise — essential for knowledge workers the way physical training is for athletes — then shares five tactics for hitting five books a month. The most important: make your phone boring. When there's no algorithmic feed competing for downtime, books become the default leisure activity. Supporting tactics include choosing varied books, scheduling reading like exercise, creating rituals, and doing "closing pushes" in the final 100 pages.

## Key Ideas
- Reading is to the brain what exercise is to the body — knowledge workers who don't read fall behind in cognitive fitness
- The single highest-leverage change: remove all interesting apps from your phone so reading becomes your default boredom activity
- Schedule reading sessions explicitly — "if I have time and feel like it" produces zero sessions
- Vary difficulty and format (audio + written, novels + nonfiction) to keep engagement high across a full month
- A "closing push" — 2-3 hours to finish the last 100 pages — prevents the trailing-off that kills completion rates
