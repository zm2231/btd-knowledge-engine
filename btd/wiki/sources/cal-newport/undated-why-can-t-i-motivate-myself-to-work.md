# Why Can't I Motivate Myself To Work?

Newport diagnoses two distinct causes of work motivation failure: deep procrastination (extrinsic goals, no intrinsic connection to the work) and dopamine sickness (brain so habituated to algorithmic stimulation that slower tasks feel impossible). He prescribes targeted solutions for each — intrinsic vision and reduced obligation load for deep procrastination; boredom therapy, interval focus training, and radical environment changes for dopamine sickness.

## Key Ideas
- Deep procrastination stems from working toward someone else's goals without a personal chain of motivation connecting the task to a desired life
- Dopamine sickness is a real neurological state: over-stimulated brains lose the ability to generate motivation for slower, demanding work
- Boredom therapy — regular periods without phone or distraction — is the primary treatment for dopamine sickness
- Interval training for focus: start with 20 minutes, add 10-minute increments as tolerance builds, just like physical training
- Radical location and ritual changes help the brain separate work from domestic context, especially when working from home
