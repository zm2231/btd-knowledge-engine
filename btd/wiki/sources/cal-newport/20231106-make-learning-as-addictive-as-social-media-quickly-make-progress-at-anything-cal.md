# Make Learning As Addictive As Social Media - Quickly Make Progress At Anything | Cal Newport

Newport argues that the dopamine feedback loops powering social media can be redesigned to power skill acquisition. The key is making progress visible and immediate — treating learning like a game with clear metrics, tight feedback cycles, and a sense of accumulation. Covers how to structure learning projects so you get regular small wins that sustain motivation, rather than the long stretches of invisibility that make self-directed learning feel defeating.

## Key Ideas
- Social media is addictive because it provides continuous, low-cost feedback signals — learning systems that mimic this retain motivation
- Making progress visible (tracking streaks, logging output, measuring improvement) closes the dopamine loop that passive study keeps open
- Structure learning as a series of achievable milestones, not one long arc — the brain responds to visible accumulation
- Deliberately timed rewards (only after completing a learning block) pair the dopamine hit with the productive behavior
- Deep Work principles apply: distraction-free sessions are where real skill accrues; scattered review accumulates nothing
