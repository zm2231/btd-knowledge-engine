# Digital Minimalism with Cal Newport

Newport's concise summary of his 2019 book. He distinguishes digital minimalism from "minimization" — it's not about using less technology, it's about using technology intentionally in service of a well-formed vision of your life. Argues that detoxes and app tweaks fail because they fight against billion-dollar attention engineering; the only sustainable counter is a positive vision that makes alternatives more compelling than scrolling.

## Key Ideas
- Digital minimalism starts with a clear picture of what you value, then works backward to choose tools that serve those values
- Detoxes fail because phones fill an existential void — stopping without replacing the void creates unbearable discomfort
- Attention engineering (infinite scroll, variable rewards, notification design) is purpose-built to beat willpower
- Behavior changes stick when they support something you positively want, not when they only avoid something bad
- The 30-day digital declutter isn't detox — it's a discovery period to rediscover what you actually care about
