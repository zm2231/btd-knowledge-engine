# Core Idea: Deep Work

Newport's definitive summary of his signature concept. Deep work = cognitively demanding tasks performed without context shifts. The video covers the three questions he always addresses: what it is, why it's rare and valuable, and how to do more of it. The core argument: in knowledge work, shallow activity keeps the lights on but deep work is what moves the needle — and it's becoming scarcer, so those who prioritize it earn disproportionate reward.

## Key Ideas
- Deep work requires both cognitive demand AND absence of context shifts — email checks during hard thinking disqualify a session
- In knowledge work, deep efforts produce the value that justifies the company's existence; shallow work just sustains operations
- Deep work's scarcity in a distracted world makes it a competitive advantage, not just a productivity hack
- Measure your deep-to-shallow ratio and set a target — what you can't measure you can't improve
- Train concentration like a muscle: scheduled blocks, rituals, and deliberate practice in cognitive discomfort
