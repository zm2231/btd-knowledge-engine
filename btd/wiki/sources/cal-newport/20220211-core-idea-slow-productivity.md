# Core Idea: Slow Productivity

Newport's response to the anti-productivity movement: not "do less and be okay with it," but a redesign of what productivity means. He traces burnout to chronic overload — too many commitments causing overhead spirals and relentless pace — then proposes three principles: do fewer things, work at a natural pace (with seasonality), and obsess over quality. The argument is that this is more compatible with human wiring than modern always-on work culture, and more sustainable long-term.

## Key Ideas
- Chronic overload short-circuits the brain's planning center, creates overhead spirals, and eliminates recovery — this is the root of burnout
- Our paleolithic wiring is optimized for skilled, important work at a natural pace with variation in intensity — not constant 10-out-of-10 days
- "Do fewer things" means fewer commitments than you can handle with ease, not zero commitments
- Natural pace requires seasonality at multiple scales: day, week, month, and year — some intense, some light
- Quality obsession makes saying no easier and earns the autonomy to take on less
