# What You'll Wish You'd Known

Written as a talk for high schoolers, this essay argues that the biggest mistake young ambitious people make is letting their need to be good at something be satisfied by being good at school. Graham's alternative: treat school as a day job, not an identity; start doing real work; work forward from promising situations rather than backward from a predetermined goal ("stay upwind"). Curiosity in adults becomes narrow and deep — a well rather than a mud flat — and that's what powers great work. The goal isn't to figure out what you want to do; it's to keep your options open while building skills and exploring.

## Key Ideas
- "Stay upwind": choose work that maximizes future options rather than committing early to a narrow goal
- The school system optimizes for impressing admissions officers, not building skills — treat it as a day job
- Curiosity is the core engine; discipline is overrated when you're genuinely interested in something
- Start doing real projects now — paths bend more than you think, and the direction becomes clear through doing
- Adults who do great work all figured out what to work on by working, not by deciding
