# Keep Your Identity Small

Graham's shortest and most concentrated essay. The reason politics and religion generate uniquely useless arguments is that they become part of people's identity — and you can't think clearly about something you've made part of yourself. The solution isn't tolerance (thinking of yourself as X but tolerating Y); it's keeping as few things as possible inside your identity, period. The more labels you carry, the dumber they make you. For builders and thinkers, this is directly actionable: don't become "an AI person" or "a founder" or "a remote-work advocate" — stay a scientist who follows the evidence.

## Key Ideas
- Identity attachment causes partisanship, not just in politics — any topic people identify with becomes undiscussable
- The solution isn't tolerance; it's actively keeping your identity small and uncommitted
- Labels make you dumber by creating commitments you didn't consciously choose
- "Scientist" is the rare identity that works because it's a placeholder, not a commitment — it just means "follow the evidence"
- If a discussion has become a religious war, it means identity got involved, not that the question has no answer
