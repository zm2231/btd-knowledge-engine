# How to Get Startup Ideas

Graham's definitive essay on startup idea generation. The central argument: don't try to think up startup ideas — that produces plausible-sounding bad ideas ("sitcom ideas"). Instead, live at the leading edge of a rapidly changing field, notice what's missing, and build it. The verb is "notice," not "think up." Good ideas are ones where the founders want it, can build it, and few others realize it's worth doing. They look like toys or schleps — easy to dismiss, hard to copy — and they start narrow and deep (a "well") rather than broad and shallow.

## Key Ideas
- "Live in the future, then build what's missing" — the most reliable path to good startup ideas
- Good startup ideas have demand shaped like a well: narrow and deep, not broad and shallow
- The schlep filter and unsexy filter are the main cognitive blocks that hide good ideas — disable them deliberately
- Being at the leading edge doesn't mean pushing it forward; being a heavy user of a changing field is enough
- The clash of domains is especially fertile: bring software expertise to a field where people haven't yet solved problems with code
