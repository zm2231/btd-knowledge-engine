# Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule

Graham distinguishes two fundamentally incompatible work rhythms: the manager's schedule (hourly blocks, switching tasks frequently, meetings are free) and the maker's schedule (half-day minimum units, a single meeting can blow a whole day). The problem is that most powerful people run on manager's schedules, so they impose their cadence on everyone — including people who need long stretches to do real work. The essay is both diagnosis and defense: knowing the difference lets makers protect their time and helps managers understand why the people building things around them seem so meeting-averse.

## Key Ideas
- For makers, a single meeting doesn't just interrupt work — it changes the mode of work and can kill an ambitious project's momentum
- Meetings are free on a manager's schedule; they're catastrophically costly on a maker's schedule
- Office hours (clustered at day's end) are the classic way to simulate manager availability without destroying maker time
- The smarter managers understand this and restrain themselves — it's not about makers being difficult, it's about incompatible time architectures
- As founders increasingly stay founders rather than becoming managers, maker schedules may become more common
