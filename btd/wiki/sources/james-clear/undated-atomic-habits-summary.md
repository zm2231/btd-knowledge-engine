# Atomic Habits Summary

The canonical summary of James Clear's *Atomic Habits* framework. Small habits (atomic = tiny + fundamental) compound into remarkable results via the same mechanism as interest. The book's central argument: most people focus on goals when they should focus on systems, and on outcomes when they should focus on identity. The four-step habit loop (cue → craving → response → reward) generates the four laws of behavior change: make it obvious, make it attractive, make it easy, make it satisfying. Inverting these laws breaks bad habits.

## Key Ideas
- 1% daily improvement compounds to 37x better in a year; 1% daily decline trends toward zero
- Goals are necessary for direction; systems are necessary for progress — goals-only thinking produces yo-yo results
- Identity is the deepest level of change: "I am a runner" sustains behavior long after motivation fades
- Four laws of behavior change: cue (make obvious), craving (make attractive), response (make easy), reward (make satisfying)
- The plateau of latent potential: results lag behind habits — most people quit during the valley of disappointment
