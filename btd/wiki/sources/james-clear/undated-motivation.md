# Motivation

A scientific guide to motivation that dismantles the "wait for inspiration" model and replaces it with a systems-based one. Clear's key insight: motivation is often a result of action, not the cause of it. Starting — even poorly — generates momentum, which generates motivation. He covers the role of dopamine in reward anticipation, why variable reward schedules are more motivating than predictable ones, how the "two-minute rule" lowers the activation energy to start, and how environment design can make motivation the default state rather than something you have to summon.

## Key Ideas
- Motivation follows action more reliably than it precedes it — start before you feel ready
- Dopamine drives anticipatory behavior (wanting), not the reward itself — the craving IS the motivation
- Variable rewards (unpredictable positive outcomes) generate more sustained engagement than predictable ones
- The two-minute rule: any habit can start with a two-minute version — getting started is the hardest part
- Environment design > willpower: making the right action the path of least resistance removes the need for motivation
