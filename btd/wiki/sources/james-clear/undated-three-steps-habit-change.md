# How to Build New Habits

Clear's practical guide to starting habits that actually stick, built around the insight that most habit attempts fail at the start because the activation energy is too high. Three keys: make the habit obvious (use cue stacking — "after I do X, I will do Y"), make it attractive (pair the habit with something you enjoy), and make it easy (never miss twice, and start with a version so small it's impossible to fail). The article also covers why "motivation" is the wrong frame and environment design is the right one.

## Key Ideas
- Habit stacking: "After [current habit], I will [new habit]" — piggybacks on existing cue-routine loops
- Never miss twice: missing once is human, missing twice is starting a new (bad) habit
- The two-minute rule: scale any habit down to a two-minute version to eliminate starting friction
- Environment design outperforms willpower — put the habit on the path of least resistance
- Systems vs. motivation: designing your environment to make good behavior automatic removes dependence on feeling ready
