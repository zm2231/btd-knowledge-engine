# Goal Setting

A scientific guide to goal-setting that builds on the research behind goal commitment, specificity, and measurement. Clear synthesizes findings on implementation intentions (the "when/where/how" specification that doubles follow-through), the importance of writing goals down, and how progress monitoring dramatically improves attainment. The article also addresses the paradox that goal-setting can reduce intrinsic motivation when done wrong — focusing purely on outcomes without a system for process can make the goal feel like a burden once achieved.

## Key Ideas
- Implementation intentions ("I will do X at time Y in place Z") roughly double habit follow-through vs. vague intentions
- Writing goals increases attainment by ~42% (Dominican University study) — the simplest high-return intervention
- Progress monitoring is independently predictive of attainment — tracking creates its own feedback loop
- Too many goals dilute attention; constraining to 2–3 quarterly goals produces more actual progress
- Goals set direction; systems determine whether you arrive — a goal without a system is a wish
