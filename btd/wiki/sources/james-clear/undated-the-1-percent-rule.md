# The 1 Percent Rule

Clear's article explaining why tiny advantages compound into disproportionate outcomes — what he calls the "1 Percent Rule." Drawing on natural selection, winner-take-most markets, and sports statistics, he argues that in any competitive system, small but consistent advantages accumulate to produce outsized differences in outcomes. Applied to personal development: the person who gets 1% better each day at their craft doesn't end up 365% better than someone who stagnates — they end up 37x better. The practical implication is that small consistent improvements matter far more than dramatic efforts.

## Key Ideas
- In winner-take-most systems, small differences in capability produce enormous differences in outcomes
- 1% better per day for one year = 37x improvement (compounding is non-linear)
- Consistency beats intensity — daily 1% gains outperform monthly 30% pushes
- The rule applies to deterioration too: 1% worse per day trends toward near-zero capability
- Focus on the trajectory, not the current position — small habits that improve the slope matter more than big one-time jumps
