# The December inflection point in AI coding agents

Karpathy argues that AI-assisted coding underwent a qualitative phase transition in December 2025 — not gradual improvement but a step function. Before December, coding agents mostly didn't work for large tasks; after, they basically do. The change came from models with higher long-term coherence and tenacity that could power through multi-file, multi-step tasks without losing the thread.

## Key Ideas
- December 2025 was a discontinuity, not a continuation — coding agents crossed a threshold where they could complete substantial tasks without constant human correction
- The key capability improvement was long-term coherence: models maintaining context and intent across large codebases and long task sequences
- This changes the economics of software development faster than any previous AI capability improvement
- The implication for builders: workflows designed for the pre-December world are now suboptimal — the right level of AI delegation has shifted significantly
