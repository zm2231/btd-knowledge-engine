# Vibe coding: one year retrospective

Karpathy reflects on the one-year anniversary of coining "vibe coding" — which he describes as a throwaway shower thought that accidentally named something a lot of people were already feeling. The retrospective is partly meta-commentary on how memes and terminology propagate through technical communities: the tweet that coined it was low-effort, unplanned, and went viral precisely because it articulated a shared but unnamed experience at the right moment.

## Key Ideas
- "Vibe coding" was coined as a description of a real practice that already existed — the name spread because it gave people vocabulary for something they were doing but couldn't describe
- Naming a practice changes how people relate to it: it legitimizes, professionalizes, and creates community around it
- The unpredictability of viral technical memes mirrors the unpredictability of ideas in general — quality and timing matter independently
- The anniversary marks a shift from "vibe coding" as a novelty to an accepted mode of software development
