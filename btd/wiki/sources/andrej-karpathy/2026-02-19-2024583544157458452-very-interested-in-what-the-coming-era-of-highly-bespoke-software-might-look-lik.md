# Bespoke software: vibe-coding a personal fitness tracker in one hour

Karpathy describes vibe-coding a custom fitness tracker app in about an hour to run a personal experiment (lower resting heart rate from 50 to 45 bpm over 8 weeks via Zone 2 cardio). The specific example grounds a broader point: when software takes an hour to build to your exact spec, the economics of "use existing app vs. build your own" inverts. Bespoke software — software that exactly fits your life — becomes accessible without programming expertise.

## Key Ideas
- Vibe coding makes bespoke personal software economical: build exactly what you need rather than adapting to what exists
- The fitness tracker example illustrates the pattern: precise personal experiment requirements don't match any off-the-shelf app
- The bottleneck shifts from technical skill to knowing what you want — the design problem becomes more important than the engineering problem
- Personal data under your own control (not in a platform's database) is a side benefit of building your own tools
