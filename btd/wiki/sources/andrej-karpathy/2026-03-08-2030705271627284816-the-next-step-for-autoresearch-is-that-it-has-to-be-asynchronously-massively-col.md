# autoresearch v2: SETI@home for AI agents

Karpathy sketches the next evolution of autoresearch: instead of a single agent iterating on one thread of commits, a distributed community of agents (like SETI@home) exploring many research directions in parallel and contributing back to a shared repo. The goal is to emulate a research community, not a single PhD student.

## Key Ideas
- Current autoresearch is serial — one agent, one research thread; the bottleneck is sequential exploration
- The SETI@home analogy: distributed agents contribute compute to a shared goal, asynchronously, without a central coordinator
- A repo becomes a "seed" that sprouts agent-contributed branches across many research directions simultaneously
- The synthesis problem (how do you merge good ideas from many branches?) is the hard part that Karpathy doesn't yet have an answer for
