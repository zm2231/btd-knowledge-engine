# Finding myself going back to RSS/Atom feeds

Karpathy argues that algorithmic social media feeds converge to the same engagement-maximizing black hole regardless of surface aesthetics, and recommends RSS as an escape hatch. His framing: any product with the same incentive structure will eventually produce the same content environment. Practical: recommends NetNewsWire, links to 92 RSS feeds of high-quality HN blogs as a cold-start list.

## Key Ideas
- Algorithmic feeds optimize for engagement, which produces slop and provocation regardless of platform identity
- RSS is open, hackable, and puts the reader in control of what they see — no algorithmic intermediary
- The signal-to-noise ratio on RSS is higher than any algorithmic feed for the same content sources
- Cold-starting an RSS reader is the biggest friction point; curated seed lists solve it
