# LLMs as simulation engines: Simile AI launch

Karpathy's angel investment announcement for Simile AI, with a substantive argument about an under-explored property of LLMs: the pretrained base model is fundamentally a simulation engine over the distribution of people on the internet, not a single personality. Most fine-tuning collapses this to one character; Simile explores keeping the distributional nature alive as a product feature.

## Key Ideas
- A pretrained LLM is not one assistant — it's a statistical model over a diverse population of writers, thinkers, and voices
- Fine-tuning typically collapses this richness into a single crafted persona, discarding information that was expensive to learn
- "Simulation engine" framing: what if you could query the model as different population segments rather than one generic assistant?
- This property could be useful for research, writing, roleplay, education, or any task where diverse perspectives are valuable
