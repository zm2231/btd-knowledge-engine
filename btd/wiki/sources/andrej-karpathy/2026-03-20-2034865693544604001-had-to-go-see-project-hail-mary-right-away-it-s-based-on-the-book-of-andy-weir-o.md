# Project Hail Mary: movie and book review

Non-technical post: Karpathy reviews the Project Hail Mary film adaptation (based on Andy Weir's novel) and the book. His enthusiasm centers on the scientific rigor of the alien design — Weir thought seriously about alternate biochemistry, evolutionary history, and sensorium. The post is personal, not professional, but reveals Karpathy's interests in rigorous first-principles thinking applied outside ML.

## Key Ideas
- The movie is faithful to the book in both content and tone — rare for a sci-fi adaptation
- Weir's alien design stands out for scientific seriousness: alternate biochemistry and evolutionary logic, not just aesthetics
- The appreciation for rigorous alien design reflects Karpathy's broader value of first-principles reasoning — asking "what would actually follow from the premises?" rather than assuming default answers
