# yup! ChatGPT makes stuff up! here's why. and what you can do about it #chatgpt #learnai

Plain-language explanation of why LLMs hallucinate — they predict probable next tokens rather than retrieve facts, so they confidently generate plausible-sounding but incorrect answers, especially on obscure topics or long reasoning chains. Balmer explains the agreeable-model problem (trained to sound helpful, not truthful) and gives practical mitigation: be specific in prompts, use prompt frameworks like RISEN, treat AI as a draft assistant not a final authority, and always verify high-stakes claims.

## Key Ideas
- LLMs are advanced autocomplete — they predict likely next words, not look up ground truth
- Training data is messy internet content; models can't reliably distinguish fact from plausible fiction
- Models are trained to be agreeable and helpful, which compounds hallucination on uncertain questions
- High-risk hallucination zones: obscure domains, evolving topics, long reasoning chains, vague prompts
- Mitigation: tight prompts, prompt frameworks, treat output as drafts, apply human expert review
