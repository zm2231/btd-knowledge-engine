# 100 hours of OpenClaw lessons in 35 minutes

Dense distillation of everything Finn learned from 100+ hours using OpenClaw (Claude's agentic interface). Covers mental models for how to interact with the agent, common failure patterns, what makes a good task description, how to use sub-agents, and when to break big projects into smaller tasks. A high-value compressed reference for anyone ramping up on agentic Claude usage.

## Key Ideas
- Task decomposition is the most important skill: one clear goal per prompt, not multi-step bundles
- Sub-agents work best when given clear scope boundaries and explicit handoff criteria
- The agent fails predictably: ambiguous instructions, missing context, and circular error loops are the top causes
- Context management matters — long conversations degrade; start fresh sessions for new tasks
- OpenClaw excels at multi-file operations and research tasks where a human would context-switch constantly
