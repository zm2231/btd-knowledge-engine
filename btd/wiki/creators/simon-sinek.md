# Simon Sinek

Leadership thinker. Author of *Start With Why* and *The Infinite Game*. His work centers on two ideas: leaders exist to take care of people, not to be in charge; and most organizations are playing the wrong game.

## Key Contributions

### [[infinite-game]] vs Finite Game
His most distinctive framework. Finite games have known rules and endpoints (baseball). Infinite games have no finish line; the goal is to keep playing. Business is an infinite game that most companies play as if it were finite. Companies that play the infinite game (Costco, Apple under Jobs) outlast and frustrate competitors who play to "win."

### [[empathy-in-leadership]]
Leadership is not about being in charge; it's about taking care of those in your charge. The Four Seasons vs Caesar's Palace story: same employee (Noah), completely different experience based on whether managers ask "how are you doing?" vs "are you doing everything right?"

### [[accountability-pattern]] (from intake relevance)
His framework for why people underperform is directly relevant to the intake interview's accountability probing. Performance problems are usually environment problems, not people problems. "Are you okay? What's going on?" vs "Your numbers are down, pick it up."
