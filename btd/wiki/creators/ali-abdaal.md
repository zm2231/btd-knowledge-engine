# Ali Abdaal

Doctor turned YouTuber. His content bridges the gap between academic research on learning/productivity and practical application. Where Karpathy teaches you WHAT to learn, Abdaal teaches you HOW to learn it and how to actually stick with it. Most useful for users whose intake profile shows accountability problems or low confidence in their ability to follow through.

## Key Contributions

### [[evidence-based-learning]]
His "How to Study for Exams" video synthesizes research on spaced repetition, active recall, and interleaving. The key insight for this project: passive consumption (watching videos, reading articles) produces almost no retention. Active engagement (testing yourself, explaining to others, applying immediately) is what works. This directly informs how experiment cards should be structured — content consumption alone is never the experiment; the exercise after consumption is.

### [[accountability-pattern]]
Abdaal's own story (Cambridge medical student, started YouTube as a side project, built systems to stay consistent) is a template for the accountability patterns the intake interview probes. His time management content isn't about willpower; it's about systems that make the default behavior productive.

### [[productivity-systems]]
Time blocking, the Eisenhower matrix adapted for real life, "feel-good productivity" (his book's thesis: productivity comes from positive emotion, not discipline). Relevant for users whose constraints include limited time — the system should recommend his frameworks alongside domain content.
