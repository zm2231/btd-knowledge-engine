# Nate Jones

AI business analyst. Covers the business side of AI that Karpathy doesn't: market dynamics, company strategy, what the big moves actually mean. His value is translating technical developments into business implications — the layer between "here's what the model can do" and "here's what that means for your career."

## Key Contributions

### [[context-engineering]]
"Prompt Engineering Is Dead, Context Engineering Is Dying — What Comes Next" is the clearest articulation of the shift from crafting individual prompts to designing entire context systems. Directly relevant for BTD members building with [[claude-code]] — understanding why CLAUDE.md and skills matter more than any single prompt.

### [[ai-agents]] — The Business Layer
Multiple videos on the agent ecosystem: OpenClaw's 160,000 developers, task queues replacing chat interfaces, the 5 levels of AI coding. Where Kashef shows you how to build agents and Mollick evaluates them, Jones explains the market dynamics — who's winning, who's consolidating, and where the opportunities are.

### [[build-in-public]] and Career Strategy
"Stop Competing with 400 Applicants — Build This in One Weekend" and "Why the Biggest AI Career Opportunity Just Appeared" are the most actionable career-positioning content in the corpus. His argument: the gap between "uses AI casually" and "builds with AI" is where the opportunity is. Connects to Newport's [[deep-work]] in an unexpected way — the skill moat is depth, not breadth.

### [[second-brain]] Systems
"You Don't Need SaaS — The $0-10 System That Replaced My AI Workflow" is a 45-minute no-code walkthrough of building personal AI infrastructure without subscription tools. Directly relevant for BTD members setting up their own knowledge systems.

### AI Market Analysis
NVIDIA economics, Amazon's 30,000 cuts, the $285B sell-off — Jones covers the financial and strategic layer of AI that nobody else in the corpus touches. Useful for users whose `goal_type` involves business, investing, or understanding where the industry is heading.
