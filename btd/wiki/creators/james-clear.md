# James Clear

Author of *Atomic Habits*. His content is the most directly actionable in the corpus for behavior change. Where Huberman explains the neuroscience and Newport gives you the system, Clear gives you the smallest possible unit of change and a framework for making it compound.

## Key Contributions

### [[atomic-habits]]
The core framework: habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. 1% better every day. The four laws of behavior change (make it obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying) are the most cited framework in the corpus for experiment design.

### Identity-Based Habits
The most distinctive idea: don't focus on what you want to achieve, focus on who you want to become. "I'm the type of person who shows up every day" is more durable than "I want to finish this course." Every experiment card in the system implicitly uses this: the experiment isn't about consuming content, it's about becoming someone who does the thing.

### The Habit Loop
Cue → routine → reward. Understanding this loop is how you design experiments that stick and diagnose why previous experiments failed. If the cue is missing, the behavior never starts. If the reward is delayed, the behavior fades.

### The 1% Rule
Connects to [[building-from-scratch]] and Karpathy's incremental approach. Small consistent improvements beat ambitious one-time efforts. This is why experiments are weekly, not monthly.

## When to Surface This Creator

- `accountability_pattern` shows any pattern of dropping things → habit loop + identity framework
- User wants to "build a habit" of anything → four laws framework
- Experiment design phase → the structure maps directly to experiment cards
- User is over-ambitious in their first experiment → 1% rule as calibration
