# Ethan Mollick

Wharton professor. His Substack (One Useful Thing) is the best resource in the corpus for non-technical people trying to understand and use AI. Where Karpathy explains how transformers work, Mollick explains what you can actually do with them at your job tomorrow. No code required.

## Key Contributions

### [[ai-for-non-technical]]
"An Opinionated Guide to Using AI Right Now" and "A Guide to Which AI to Use in the Agentic Era" are the canonical starting points. He tells you which tool to use for what, without the marketing hype. Practical, current, opinionated. If someone's intake shows they want to use AI but their `calibrated_level` is pre-beginner, Mollick is the on-ramp — not Karpathy, not 3B1B.

### [[llm-limitations]]
"The Shape of AI: Jaggedness, Bottlenecks and Salients" introduces his most useful framework: AI capabilities are jagged, not smooth. A model might be brilliant at writing marketing copy and terrible at basic arithmetic. You can't assume competence transfers across tasks. This directly connects to Simon Willison's [[vibe-coding]] warnings — the same model that writes beautiful code might silently introduce security holes.

### Management as AI Superpower
His argument that managers get disproportionate value from AI because they already know what good output looks like and can evaluate it quickly. Non-managers need to develop this [[taste]]. Relevant for users in leadership roles — connects to Sinek's leadership content from a completely different angle.

### [[claude-code]] and What Comes Next
Practical analysis of agentic coding tools. His "Claude Code and What Comes Next" and "Claude Dispatch and the Power of Interfaces" pieces are the most accessible explanations of why [[ai-agents]] matter for non-engineers. Relevant for any BTD member working on projects who wants to understand the tool they're using without reading the Anthropic docs.

### [[ai-agents]] in Practice
"Real AI Agents and Real Work" and "Giving Your AI a Job Interview" cut through the agent hype. His framing: most "agents" are just chatbots with extra steps; real agents need real evaluation. The job interview metaphor — test your AI the way you'd test a new hire — is the most practical agent evaluation framework in the corpus.

## When to Surface This Creator

- `calibrated_level` is pre-beginner or beginner in AI → start here, not Karpathy
- `goal_type` is "apply AI at work" → opinionated guide + jaggedness framework
- User is a manager → management as AI superpower
- User asks "which AI tool should I use" → his comparison guides
- User's `blind_spot` is overestimating AI consistency → jaggedness framework
