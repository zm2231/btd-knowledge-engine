# Ethan Mollick

Wharton professor. His Substack (One Useful Thing) is the best resource in the corpus for non-technical people trying to understand and use AI. Where Karpathy explains how transformers work, Mollick explains what you can actually do with them at your job tomorrow. No code required.

## Key Contributions

### AI for Non-Technical Professionals
"An Opinionated Guide to Using AI Right Now" and "A Guide to Which AI to Use in the Agentic Era" are the canonical starting points. He tells you which tool to use for what, without the marketing hype. Practical, current, opinionated.

### The Jaggedness of AI
"The Shape of AI: Jaggedness, Bottlenecks and Salients" introduces his most useful framework: AI capabilities are jagged, not smooth. A model might be brilliant at writing marketing copy and terrible at basic arithmetic. You can't assume competence transfers across tasks. This is critical for users whose `goal_type` is "apply AI at work."

### Management as AI Superpower
His argument that managers get disproportionate value from AI because they already know what good output looks like and can evaluate it quickly. Non-managers need to develop this taste. Relevant for users in leadership roles.

### Claude Code and What Comes Next
Practical analysis of agentic coding tools. Relevant for any user interested in building with AI, especially those in the BTD group working on projects.

## When to Surface This Creator

- `calibrated_level` is pre-beginner or beginner in AI → start here, not Karpathy
- `goal_type` is "apply AI at work" → opinionated guide + jaggedness framework
- User is a manager → management as AI superpower
