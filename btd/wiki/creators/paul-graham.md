# Paul Graham

YC co-founder. His essays are the canonical reference for how builders think about starting things, choosing what to work on, and doing it well. Dense, opinionated, and specific. Every essay is short enough to read in one sitting but substantial enough to change how you think about the topic.

## Key Contributions

### How to Do Great Work
The longest and most comprehensive essay. Core argument: great work comes from working on what genuinely interests you, being prolific, and being willing to look foolish. "The bus ticket theory of genius" connects: obsessive interest in a topic (even an unfashionable one) is a better predictor of breakthrough work than intelligence.

### [[startup-ideas]]
"How to Get Startup Ideas" is the best single piece on finding things to build. The key insight: the best startup ideas come from noticing problems you personally have, not from trying to think of startup ideas. "Live in the future and build what's missing." Directly relevant for any user whose `goal_type` is "build."

### Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule
Two types of people have two types of days. Makers need long uninterrupted blocks. Managers schedule in one-hour slots. The clash between them destroys productivity. Connects directly to Newport's deep work: if your calendar looks like a manager's schedule, you can't do deep work.

### Identity
"Keep Your Identity Small" — the more labels you attach to yourself, the harder it is to think clearly about things those labels touch. Connects to James Clear's identity-based habits from the opposite direction: Clear says build identity intentionally. Graham says beware of identity you've accumulated accidentally.
