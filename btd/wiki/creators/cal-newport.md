# Cal Newport

Computer science professor at Georgetown. Author of *Deep Work*, *Digital Minimalism*, and *Slow Productivity*. His content is the most prescriptive in the corpus; where Abdaal explains why learning science works, Newport gives you the exact scheduling system to implement it.

## Key Contributions

### [[deep-work]]
His core framework. Deep work is focusing without distraction on a cognitively demanding task. It's not about working more hours; it's about protecting the hours where real thinking happens. A 40-hour time-blocked week produces the same output as a 60-hour unstructured week. The video "Core Idea: Deep Work" is the best single-video summary.

### [[productivity-systems]]
Time blocking, the shutdown ritual, the planning system. His method: every minute of your day has a job. Not rigid scheduling; adaptive planning where you decide in advance what block gets what type of work. "Cal Newport's Planning System (In Detail)" walks through the full implementation.

### [[digital-minimalism]]
The argument for deliberately choosing your digital tools rather than defaulting to whatever everyone else uses. Relevant for users whose intake reveals "I can't focus" or "I spend all day on my phone" patterns.

### Slow Productivity
Three principles: do fewer things, work at a natural pace, obsess over quality. The opposite of hustle culture. Relevant for users who over-scope then abandon.
