# Beware The Defaults

The community's own publication — [bewarethedefault.substack.com](https://bewarethedefault.substack.com). Primary voice for the BTD thesis: most people are running on autopilot, and the builders who notice their defaults are the ones who ship. Eight articles in the corpus covering the founding argument, the death of how-to content, building in public, and the AI skills nobody's practicing.

## Key Contributions

### [[defaults]] — The Founding Thesis
"The Silent Killer of the AI Era" introduces the BTD concept directly: defaults are the invisible scripts running your decisions. The bankrupt-CEO story is the sharpest illustration in the corpus of what a default failure looks like at scale. Every other BTD piece builds on this one.

### [[build-in-public]] — The Garage Door Open
"Building with the Garage Door Open" is the community's manifesto for learning in public. The argument: polished demos teach polish, messy problem-solving teaches judgment. Friday Build Labs are the practice arm of this idea. The piece that introduced the meta-principle BTD uses for its own community events.

### [[vibe-coding]] + [[skills-as-workflows]] — The AI Skill Nobody's Practicing
The most recent piece argues that the scarce skill isn't prompting — it's encoding your process. As how-to content dies (anyone can generate a tutorial now), the value shifts to judgment: knowing which process to encode and when to deviate from it.

### [[taste]] + [[build-in-public]] — Lights, Camera, Friction
Reframes the friction of building publicly as the mechanism for developing taste. You can't develop taste without feedback loops. Friction is the feedback.
