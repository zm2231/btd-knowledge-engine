# Lenny Rachitsky

Former Airbnb PM, now runs the biggest product management newsletter/podcast. His podcast guests are the people actually shipping products at top companies. His Substack articles are the synthesis layer — where he takes what he learns from guests and turns it into actionable frameworks.

## Key Contributions

### [[claude-code]] in Production
"I Gave Claude Code Our Entire Codebase — Our Customers Noticed" (Al Chen, Galileo) is the most concrete case study in the corpus of a company using Claude Code on real production code with real customer impact. Not a demo, not a tutorial — an actual deployment story with outcomes. Complements Kashef's setup tutorials with the "what happens after you set it up" perspective.

### [[ai-agents]] — The Product Lens
"OpenClaw: The Complete Guide" and "A Power User's Guide to the Most Powerful Personal AI Tool" cover agents from a product management perspective — not how they work technically, but how to evaluate them, how to train them, and when they're worth the investment. This is the product counterpart to Kashef's technical walkthroughs and Mollick's [[ai-for-non-technical|academic analysis]].

### [[build-in-public]] — Creative Recovery
"A Visual Guide to Getting Out of a Creative Slump" is the most human piece in the corpus about what happens when building momentum stalls. Relevant for BTD members whose `accountability_pattern` shows start-stop cycles.

### [[vibe-coding]] at Company Scale
The AI State of the Union piece and the Anthropic growth episode (Amol Avasare) cover what happens when vibe-coding meets organizational scale. Anthropic is "growing itself at this point" — their own tools building their own product. Connects to Willison's inflection point framing but from the business growth angle.

### [[startup-ideas]] and Product Strategy
Community wisdom pieces on evaluating startup equity, navigating pre-seed fundraising, and [[mcp-servers]] as product infrastructure. The Clay/Slack inbox episode shows what [[build-in-public]] looks like for internal tools — building custom infrastructure because the off-the-shelf version doesn't fit.
