# Sabrina Ramonov

The BTD creator who speaks directly to the vibe-coder and the builder-without-a-CS-degree. Where Mark Kashef shows you what to click and type, Ramonov tells you why the path exists — and how to stay on it. Her background is computer science and physics, she built and sold an AI company for millions of dollars, and now she teaches AI for free. That combination gives her an unusual position: technically grounded enough to show real workflows, practically motivated enough to keep the frame on shipping and earning, not credentials. Her content is explicitly targeted at people who are overwhelmed, not sure where to start, and watching others appear to make money while they spin out. She names that experience directly, then solves it.

## Key Contributions

### [[vibe-coding]] and AI-Assisted Building
"AI Vibe Coding Video Games with Cursor AI, Claude Sonnet 3.7, and Three.js" and "Claude Code Tutorial for Beginners" both demonstrate building real, runnable things without a technical background as the prerequisite. The beginner tutorial is 35 minutes covering Claude Code installation, first MCP server setup, and building an AI social media assistant end-to-end. Her frame: Claude Code is "the AI tool of the year — imagine ChatGPT, but with the ability to actually get real work done." She's explicit about the target user: non-technical, willing to follow steps, wanting a real output at the end.

### [[skills-as-workflows]] — Claude Skills as Leverage
"CLAUDE CODE FULL COURSE" (72 minutes) is the most systems-oriented piece in her corpus. It walks through building an AI marketing officer from scratch: install Claude Code, create two custom skills, set up quality gates with hooks, encode brand voice, and build a multi-platform publishing pipeline using parallel sub-agents. The endpoint is a system where you type one sentence and Claude researches a topic, drafts a week's worth of content across formats, generates visuals, shows you the plan for approval, and schedules everything to social. She frames skills not as a clever Claude trick but as the mechanism for having leverage — doing more without adding headcount or hours.

### [[prompt-design]] — Practical Prompting Without the Jargon
"3 Simple AI Prompts for Genius Answers" is a tight 5-minute demonstration of three prompt suffixes that systematically improve any LLM output: (1) "Ask me clarifying questions until you're 95% confident you can complete the task successfully" — forces the model to surface what you haven't thought about; (2) "What would a top 0.1% person in this field think?" — credited to Ethan Mollick, routes the model through expert-level evaluation; (3) "Reframe this in a way that challenges how I see the problem" — breaks the AI's default toward agreement. "12 Ways to Use Claude So Well It Feels Illegal" covers the full prompt engineering mental model as one of twelve productivity patterns, making the point that you don't need a $5,000 course — you need a simple template that covers 99% of real use.

### [[ai-agents]] — N8N Automation and Multi-Platform Publishing
"This Workflow Auto Posts in 7 Different Socials (Free Template)" is an 18-minute walkthrough of an N8N workflow that: monitors Hacker News daily, selects the most viral-potential story, writes a 30-second video script, creates an AI avatar video via Hey Gen with an 11Labs voice, and publishes to Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, Threads, LinkedIn, and Twitter. The template is freely downloadable. "Claude Just Changed Content Creation Forever! (Tutorial)" extends this to Claude Code + Remotion: creating motion graphics videos, inserting web screenshots, editing existing footage, and scheduling through the Blotato MCP. Her own content operation — approaching 2 million followers across platforms without a team — is the proof of concept she's demonstrating live.

### The 8 AI Skills Framework
"The 8 AI Skills That Will Separate Winners From Losers in 2026" (35 minutes) is Ramonov's most strategic piece. She structures AI fluency as a layered skill stack: foundational mindset skills that apply regardless of AI (first three), then AI-specific skills (prompt engineering, building AI systems, using AI for content and distribution), then business-building skills (offer design, monetization). The argument is that the people visibly winning with AI aren't doing anything technically out of reach — they're doing eight specific things that most people skip or sequence wrong. The video is a good first-watch for any BTD member who feels behind and wants a map.

### 1-Person AI Business Playbook
"How To Start A 1-Person AI Business ($0 to $1M)" is the business-building companion to the skills video. Three-step playbook: (1) Lock into one AI sub-niche for a full year, no switching; (2) Learn in public — post five times a day while you're still learning, sharing what works and what doesn't; (3) Launch an information product or school community in that niche, priced for recurring revenue. She saw one entrepreneur execute this and go from zero to $50K/month in six months building a community around a single AI skill. The structural insight: most failures come from doing things in the wrong order, not from a bad idea.
