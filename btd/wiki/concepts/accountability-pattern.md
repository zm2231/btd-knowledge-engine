# Accountability Pattern

What happens when someone tries to do something hard and it stops going well. Everyone has a pattern. The intake interview surfaces it; the experiment design accounts for it.

## Common Patterns

**Starts strong, drops at first difficulty.** Week 1 is exciting. Week 3, the material gets hard or life intervenes, and they stop. Most online course completion rates are under 10% because of this pattern.

**Blames the tool.** "I just need the right app / course / framework." Cycles through tools instead of doing the work with any of them.

**Over-scopes then abandons.** Plans a 6-month project, gets overwhelmed by the scope, never starts or quits after a few days.

**Works alone, never asks for help.** Gets stuck, doesn't know who to ask, sits with the problem until motivation dies.

## How the System Uses This

The constraint profile captures `accountability_pattern` during intake. This changes experiment design:

- **Drops at first difficulty** → smaller experiments, quicker wins, Abdaal's evidence-based study methods
- **Blames the tool** → experiment focuses on one tool, removes the option to switch
- **Over-scopes** → experiment is explicitly time-boxed to one week, one deliverable
- **Works alone** → experiment includes a social component (explain to someone, send to a colleague)

## Sinek's Frame

It's not always the person. Sometimes the environment is wrong. "Are you okay? What's going on?" before "your numbers are down." The check-in flow in RE-ENTRY.md asks "was the experiment wrong, or was the execution wrong?" — same distinction.
