# Empathy in Leadership

Sinek's distinction: empathy is concern for the human being, not their output.

## The Two Conversations

**Without empathy**: "Your numbers are down for the third quarter. Pick it up or I can't guarantee your future."
**With empathy**: "Your numbers are down for the third quarter. Are you okay? I'm worried about you. What's going on?"

Same data. Completely different leadership. The first creates fear. The second creates trust.

## The Four Seasons Test

Noah works at both Four Seasons and Caesar's Palace. Same person.
- At Four Seasons: "I love my job." Managers walk past and ask how he's doing, if he needs anything.
- At Caesar's Palace: "I keep my head down and get through the day." Managers try to catch people doing things wrong.

The insight: it's not the people, it's the environment. Leaders who blame their team for underperformance are usually describing their own leadership failure.

## Connection to the Intake Interview

The intake skill probes `accountability_pattern` — what happens when someone drops things. Sinek's framework suggests the answer isn't always about the person. Sometimes the environment (tools, time, support) is wrong. The check-in flow in RE-ENTRY.md specifically asks "was the experiment wrong, or was the execution wrong?" — this is the empathy distinction applied to learning.
