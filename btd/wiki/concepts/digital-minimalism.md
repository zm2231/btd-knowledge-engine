# Digital Minimalism

Not "use your phone less." A philosophy of technology use: start with a clear vision of the life you want, then work backwards to decide what technology serves that vision — and ignore everything else.

Newport's definition is intentional about what it *isn't*: it's not minimization (less tech = better). It's intention. A digital minimalist uses technology enthusiastically — but on their own terms, not as a passive participant in someone else's attention economy.

## Why Detoxes Don't Work

Two failed responses to phone overuse:
1. **Detox** — white-knuckle abstinence. Fails because the phone was filling a genuine psychological need. Remove it cold turkey and you're left staring into an existential void most people aren't prepared for.
2. **Tweaking** — better content moderation, time-tracking apps, grayscale mode. Fails because you're fighting a multi-billion dollar machine designed specifically to defeat willpower at scale.

Both responses try to avoid a negative. Newport's research: behavior changes that pursue a positive vision are far more sustainable than those that flee a negative habit.

## The Framework

1. **Define what you value.** What do you actually want your life to look like? What activities feel meaningful? This is the prerequisite step most people skip.
2. **Work backwards.** For each thing you value, ask: what's the best way technology can support this? Not "should I use this app" — "does this tool serve this specific thing I care about?"
3. **Put rules around your use.** If you know *why* you're using a tool, the rules become obvious. Facebook for a cycling group → browser plugin that strips the news feed, only on desktop, never on phone.
4. **Opt out of everything else.** You don't need to audit every new platform. "Not my job to figure out why the Apple Watch is useful to me." Your plan evolves slowly; new tech doesn't interrupt it.

## The 30-Day Declutter

For people who don't know what they value (because the phone has been filling that space): step away from all optional technology for 30 days. Not as a detox — as a space to rediscover what matters. What you actually want to do with your time. What feels meaningful when you're not being algorithmically pacified.

Come back from those 30 days knowing what you're bringing back and why.

## For BTD Builders

The digital minimalism lens applies to tools and workflows, not just social media. The same question — "what is the best way to use this tool to support what I'm trying to do?" — applies to AI tools, Claude Code, notification settings, anything. Most builders accumulate tools the same way people accumulate apps. Digital minimalism is the pruning philosophy.

The productive version of this question for a vibe coder: *"What's the minimum set of tools that let me build what I need to build, used in the way that actually helps rather than distracts?"*
