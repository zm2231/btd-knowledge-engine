# Evidence-Based Learning

The research on how humans actually retain information, as synthesized by Abdaal from cognitive science literature. The core finding: most people's default study methods (re-reading, highlighting, passive watching) are almost useless for long-term retention. The methods that work feel harder in the moment but produce dramatically better results.

## The Three Pillars

### Active Recall
Testing yourself produces stronger memories than re-reading the material. After watching a video or reading an article, close it and try to write down what you learned. The struggle to remember IS the learning. This is why the experiment card template includes exercises after content consumption, not just the consumption itself.

### Spaced Repetition
Reviewing material at increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days) is far more effective than cramming. Each review strengthens the memory trace. For this project: experiments are weekly, and the check-in at the end of each week is a form of spaced review.

### Interleaving
Mixing different types of problems or topics during study (rather than blocking one topic at a time) produces better transfer learning. You confuse yourself more in the moment but generalize better. For this project: experiments should mix content types (a video + an exercise + an application) rather than "watch 5 videos this week."

## Connection to Experiment Design

Every experiment card should reflect these principles:
- Content consumption is never the whole experiment (passive watching ≠ learning)
- The exercise after consumption is where learning happens (active recall)
- The observable outcome should test whether the person can DO something, not just whether they consumed the content
- Weekly experiments with check-ins naturally create spaced repetition

## When to Surface

From the constraint profile:
- `accountability_pattern` shows "starts courses, drops by week 3" → the person is probably using passive methods that don't produce visible progress, so they lose motivation
- `calibrated_level` is beginner → teach HOW to learn before teaching WHAT to learn
- `goal_type` is "learn" → learning science is foundational to every experiment
